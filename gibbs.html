Thermodynamics

The study of energy and how it moves.
The physics of heat, work, and temperature

State Functions

Formula: G = H - TS

Units: all forms of energy (J, kJ, etc)
Description: Gibbs free energy combines enthalpy and entropy to predict whether a process will occur spontaneously at constant temperature and pressure. It is a measure of the maximum amount of useful work that can be obtained from a system at a constant temperature and pressure.
Use: Used for chemical reactions since it combines the usefulness of Enthalpy and Entropy to predict whether a reaction can happen.
