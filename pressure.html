Thermodynamics

The study of energy and how it moves.
The physics of heat, work, and temperature

State Functions

Formula: PV = nRT

Units: Pascals, Bar, ATM
Description: Pressure is the force exerted per unit area and is typically measured in units such as Pascals (Pa) or atmospheres (atm). In a gas, pressure is caused by the collisions of gas molecules with the walls of their container. In a fluid, pressure arises from the weight of the fluid above a given point.
Uses: Pressure is a key parameter in thermodynamics with diverse applications across various fields. In gasses, pressure reflects the force exerted by particles on their container, serving as a crucial factor in gas laws that describe behaviors under different conditions. In thermodynamic processes, pressure plays a pivotal role in determining the work done by or on a system, influencing the efficiency and performance of engines and other devices. Understanding pressure changes is essential in studying phase transitions, such as the compression or expansion of gasses, contributing to advancements in refrigeration and industrial processes. Additionally, pressure considerations are vital in the analysis of fluid dynamics, weather patterns, and material properties, making it a fundamental aspect in the broader context of thermodynamics.
