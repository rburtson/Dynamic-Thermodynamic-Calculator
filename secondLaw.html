Thermodynamics

The study of energy and how it moves.
The physics of heat, work, and temperature

All heat can not be converted to work. Irreversibility (see Entropy)

Entropy: Entropy is often defined as the “randomness” or “disorder” of systems as its value if often tied to the amount of states that the system is able to be in at a given moment in time. When viewing the universe itself as a system we can see that the entropy always increases for all possible thermodynamic processes. Entropy governs the flow of energy of a system, it is the reason heat does spontaneously go from cold temperatures to hot ones.
