Thermodynamics

The study of energy and how it moves.
The physics of heat, work, and temperature

State Functions

Formula: T = PV/R

Units: °K, °C
Description: Temperature is a measure of the average kinetic energy of the particles in a substance. It determines the direction of heat transfer between two objects when they are in contact. Temperature is typically measured in units such as degrees Celsius (°C) or Kelvin (K), where 0 K is absolute zero, the lowest possible temperature.
