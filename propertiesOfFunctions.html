State Functions

What are state functions?

One of the most powerful tools in thermodynamics, state functions are snapshots that tell us the current condition of a system at any given moment. These variables can include anything from temperature and pressure in a gas, to the amount of energy or matter within a system. By knowing the state variables, we can understand and predict how a system behaves under different conditions. You can define and calculate any state variable with any other two.
